ABOUT ME

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Information Systems at Boston University Questrom School of Business (expected 2026) and am also affiliated with the Digital Business Institute at Questrom. I am advised by Professor Dokyun Lee and am a member of the BIT LAB.

My research interests are broadly in the application, innovation and impact of AI and frontier technologies on society and in the future of work. In particular, I am interested in:

  • Computational creativity
  • Generative AI
  • Human-AI collaboration
  • Economics of unstructured data

I hold a Bachelor degree from Washington University in St. Louis (2018), a Master of Business Administration from Carnegie Mellon Tepper School of Business, and a Master of Science in Business Administration from Washington University in St. Louis (2023). Prior to my PhD studies, I worked as a marketing consultant for a large market research firm and as a Machine Learning contractor for a non-profit organization.

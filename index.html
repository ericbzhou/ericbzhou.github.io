ABOUT ME

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Information Systems at Boston University Questrom School of Business (expected 2026) and am also affiliated with the Digital Business Institute at Questrom. I am advised by Professor Dokyun Lee and am a member of the BIT LAB.

My research interests are broadly the application, innovation and impact of AI on platforms and in the future of work. In particular, I am interested in:

  • Computational creativity
  • Generative AI
  • Human-machine collaborative systems
  • Economics of unstructured data

I hold a Bachelor's degree from Washington University in St. Louis (2018), a Master's degree in Business Administration from Carnegie Mellon University (2021), and a Master's degree of Science in Business Administration from Washington University in St. Louis (2023). Prior to my PhD studies, I worked as a marketing consultant for a large market research firm and as a Machine Learning contractor for a non-profit organization.

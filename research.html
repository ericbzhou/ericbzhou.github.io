RESEARCH STATEMENT

My academic objectives are driven by a fundamental question: how are human and artificial intelligence (AI) co-evolving?

I am drawn to questions about how generative AI can facilitate human augmentation by empowering individuals and their intellectual capabilities, rather than driving automation that diminishes human agency. I focus on creativity – a faculty that is a uniquely human form of intelligence and expression - because it underpins innovation, problem-solving, and self-expression. My passion for understanding how human creativity and markets for artists are responding to the introduction of generative technologies was fostered through discussions with several of my closest friends who work in creative fields.

My research agenda primarily centers around two interrelated themes:

  • Societal consequences of generative AI
  • Human creativity and creative markets in response to AI

I am also secondarily interested in:

  • Multi-agent systems to simulate social processes
  • Design and analysis of human-AI interfaces in healthcare

I apply a combination of deep learning, Large Language Models, multimodal feature extraction, and causal inference methods to large-scale datasets to derive insights from unstructured data, specializing in working with image data.

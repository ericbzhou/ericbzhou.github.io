WORKS IN PROGRESS

  1. Eric Zhou, Xiang Hui, and Dokyun Lee. “Economic Value of Image-Based Seller Quality Signals.”

    In online marketplaces, sellers can rely on alternative mechanisms to signal their quality when they lack rich transaction histories. Using scraped data on GPU sales from eBay, we find that certain image signals can substitute for reputation to increase conversion rates amongst sellers with less than 100% positive reputation, and conditional on making a sale, can realize a 5% price premium on average. However, the effects are only significant for less reputable sellers.

  2. Eric Zhou and Dokyun Lee. “Generative AI, Human Creativity, and Art.”

    In recent years, artificial intelligence (AI) has demonstrated its ability to produce outputs that society traditionally considers "creative". One such AI system is text-to-image generative AI, which automates parts of the creative process to generate high-quality digital artworks. However, it is important to examine whether this technology truly enhances human creativity or simply facilitates the mass production of generic content. To assess the impact of text-to-image generative AI, we investigate its adoption on a prominent art-sharing platform. We collect a dataset of over 4 million artworks from more than 50,000 unique users, including over 3,000 AI adopters. Initial findings indicate that the adoption of text-to-image algorithms increases human creative productivity by over 50% compared to pre-adoption outputs. Moreover, the perceived value of their creative works nearly doubles. More to come!

RESEARCH STATEMENT

My academic objectives are driven by a fundamental question: how are human and artificial intelligence (AI) co-evolving?

I am drawn to questions about how generative AI can facilitate human augmentation by empowering individuals and their intellectual capabilities, rather than driving automation that diminishes human agency. I focus on creativity – a faculty that is a uniquely human form of intelligence and expression - because it underpins innovation, problem-solving, and self-expression. My interest in understanding how human creativity and markets for artists are responding to the introduction of generative technologies was fostered through discussions with several of my closest friends who work in creative fields.

My research agenda primarily centers around two interrelated themes:

  • Societal consequences of generative AI
  • Human creativity and creative markets in response to AI

I am secondarily interested in:

  • Multi-agent systems to simulate social processes
  • Design and analysis of human-AI collaborative systems

I combine theoretical and philosophical perspectives on human creativity with causal inference and cutting-edge empirical tools like Deep Learning, Large Language Models (LLMs), and multimodal feature extraction to understand the impacts of AI on human creativity and creative markets. My research reaches far beyond just a management audience; it synthesizes perspectives from philosophy, psychology, and AI to shed light on one of the most pressing issues today: how generative AI will impact the most 'human' aspects of society.
